The Melbage Cup Golfing Society was established in 1991 when 20 golfers took the Lugsy Balone Hackers Association on tour to Ilfracombe, North Devon. Previously a pairs event, the 2 round Lugsy Balone tournament was eclipsed when the 20 players divided into 2 teams to play for "The Melbage Cup".

The tour became an annual match play event, and was increased to 24 players to emulate the workings of The Ryder Cup. The tour has since visited Minehead, Bournemouth, Bude and Eastbourne, and in 2000 as part of the millennium celebrations went to Vilamoura in Portugal.

The MGA World Tour is a year long stableford competition. Melbage Cup players compete throughout the year in groups as small as two-balls, their cards being logged and assessed to form ongoing Order of Merit positions as well as Greens in Regulation, Putting Statistics, Driving Accuracy, and an analysis of Lost Balls (Ferrets).
