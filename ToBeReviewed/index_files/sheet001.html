MGA World Tour The Melbage Cup Golfing Society was established in 1992 when 20 golfers took the Lugsy Balone Hackers Association on tour to Ilfracombe, North Devon. Previously a pairs event, the 2 round Lugsy Balone tournament was eclipsed when the 20 players divided into 2 teams to play for "The Melbage Cup".
Current Stats
Season 18 Statistics  The tour became an annual match play event, and was increased to 24 players to emulate the workings of The Ryder Cup. The tour has since visited Minehead, Bournemouth, Bude, Bognor Regis and Eastbourne, and in 2000 as part of the millennium celebrations went to Vilamoura in Portugal. In May 2011 the Melbage tour celebrated it's 20th anniversary with a second trip overseas to Marbella, Spain. 12 of those who played in the 20th anniversary tour were members of the original 20 who played in the very first Melbage Cup.

Majors Trophy 2016/17 The MGA World Tour is a year long stableford competition. Melbage Cup players compete throughout the year in groups as small as two-balls, their cards being logged and assessed to form ongoing Order of Merit positions as well as Greens in Regulation, Putting Statistics, Driving Accuracy, and an analysis of Lost Balls (Ferrets).
